The Worlds of Our Solar
System

Our solar system is home to fascinating planets — each
one unique in its composition, atmosphere, and mysteries.

Name : Mercury

Type : Rocky planet Moons: 0 Day length: 59 Earth days Average temperature: 167°C

Mercury is the smallest planet in our Solar System and the one closest to the Sun. Because it has almost no atmosphere, its surface experiences extreme temperature changes — scorching hot during the day and freezing cold at night. Covered in craters and rocky plains, Mercury looks very similar to Earth’s Moon. Scientists study it to learn how rocky planets like ours were formed billions of years ago.

Name : Venus

Type : Rocky planet Moons: 0 Day length: 243 Earth days Average temperature: 464°C

Venus is the brightest planet in the night sky and is often called Earth’s twin because of its similar size and composition. However, its thick atmosphere, made mostly of carbon dioxide, traps heat and creates a greenhouse effect strong enough to melt lead. Constant volcanic activity and clouds of sulfuric acid make Venus one of the most hostile worlds in the Solar System.

Name : Earth

Type : Rocky planet Moons: 1 (The Moon) Day length: 24 hours Average temperature: 15°C

Earth is the only known planet that supports life. It has liquid water, a protective atmosphere, and a magnetic field that shields it from harmful solar radiation. Covered by oceans, forests, mountains, and deserts, our planet provides a perfect balance for millions of species to survive. Earth’s diversity and beauty make it a rare and fragile oasis in the vastness of space.

Name : Saturn

Type : Gas giant Moons: 146 known moons Day length: 10.7 hours Average temperature: -138°C

Saturn is easily recognized for its stunning system of rings made of ice and rock particles. It’s a planet of beauty and mystery, with many unique moons — including Titan, which has a thick atmosphere and liquid methane lakes. Despite being mostly made of gas, Saturn plays an important role in balancing the Solar System’s gravitational structure.

Name : Neptune

Type : Ice giant Moons: 14 known moons Day length: 16 hours Average temperature: -201°C

Neptune is the farthest planet from the Sun and one of the most dynamic. Its deep blue color and swirling clouds hide powerful winds that can reach up to 2,000 km/h — the fastest in the Solar System. Although distant and frozen, Neptune remains an object of fascination, offering clues about the formation and evolution of outer planets.

Name : Pluto

Type : Dwarf planet Moons: 5 (including Charon) Day length: 6.4 Earth days Average temperature: -229°C

Once considered the ninth planet, Pluto is now classified as a dwarf planet. It lies in the Kuiper Belt, a region filled with icy bodies beyond Neptune. Despite its small size, Pluto has a complex surface with mountains of ice, vast plains, and a thin atmosphere that freezes and thaws as it moves closer to or farther from the Sun. Its largest moon, Charon, is so big that the two bodies almost orbit each other like a double planet.
